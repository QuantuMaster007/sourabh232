What this dashboard solves

  • Single source of truth for operational health: delivery performance, backlog risk, expedite spend, and quality holds.
  • Faster weekly business reviews by replacing manual spreadsheets with standardized KPI tiles + drilldowns.
  • Actionability: highlights top drivers (parts/suppliers/programs) and shows where to focus.

KPI tiles (example)

These are the standard top-level KPIs (you can adjust to match your actual dashboard).

KPI Definition Why it matters
OTIF % On-time AND in-full delivery rate. Measures true customer delivery performance.
Past Due $ Open qty × unit price where need-by < today. Quantifies backlog risk in dollars.
Expedite Spend Freight cost where expedite_flag = true. Shows cost of schedule recovery.
Avg Commit Slip Delivery date − promise date (avg days). Tracks reliability of supplier commits.
Reschedule Rate % lines with reschedule_count > 0. Signals planning churn and instability.
Quality Hold Rate % lines with quality_hold_flag = true. Captures blocked inventory due to quality issues.

Data model (simple)

Describe your tables and relationships briefly so it looks “real” and credible.

  • FactSupply: one row per PO line / supply line (need-by date, promise date, qty, price, expedite flag, quality hold).
  • DimDate: calendar dates for time intelligence (weekly/monthly trends).
  • DimSupplier / DimPart (optional): master data for slicing.

Example relationship: DimDate[Date] → FactSupply[need_by_date] (many-to-one).

How to use (WBR flow)

  1. Start with KPI tiles: identify if OTIF or Past Due $ is off-track.
  2. Use trends: confirm if issue is new or persistent.
  3. Drill into drivers: supplier/part/program breakdown.
  4. Log actions: expedite / reschedule / supplier escalation.
